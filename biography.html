Olga Lockenkopf

Olga Lockenkopf, a Swiss composer and versatile artist, was born in 1979 in Istanbul. She spent her childhood there before moving to Zurich in her early teens, where she was profoundly influenced by her parents—leading linguists and archaeologists specializing in antiquity and Arabic studies. Her older brother is now a prominent journalist and foreign correspondent in Japan.

From an early age, Olga displayed extraordinary musical talent, especially on the piano and harmonica, earning early recognition as a prodigy. At the age of eleven, she composed her first piece: a musical setting of Shakespeare’s Sonnet No. 7.

Her artistic development was shaped significantly by the Viennese School and the Turkish compositional tradition of the 1960s and 1970s. In addition, Olga draws inspiration from the folk music of Greece, Turkey, Portugal, Central Asia, and Russia. The influence of the Soviet-Russian compositional school is also palpable in her work.

Today, Olga lives and works independently as an artist in Vienna. Her creative output is often marked by a deep sense of nostalgia for Saint Petersburg, her favorite city, whose atmosphere plays a central role in many of her compositions.

Beyond her musical endeavors, Olga is also a distinguished visual artist, with a focus on painting ceramics. She loves to spend time by the sea, where she—an award-winning swimmer—drifts between the waves, drawing new inspiration for her artistic pursuits. Greece in particular provides her with an ideal retreat, although she occasionally longs for her birthplace, Turkey.

Contrary to today’s societal trend toward ever more visibility, Olga prefers to dedicate herself to her artistic work in seclusion. Her lifestyle is founded on the belief that true depth in music and art can only emerge through wholehearted devotion in private reflection on the world, on oneself, and in sincere exchange with close friends.
